History & Mythology

“Nav-ratri” means Nine Nights - dedicated to Shakti, the Divine Feminine. In the Devi Mahatmya, the Goddess (Durga/Ambe Maa) battles and defeats the buffalo-demon Mahishasura over nine nights; the tenth day is Vijayadashami (victory of good over evil). In Gujarat, devotion meets dance: communities gather nightly to sing aarti and dance Garba around the symbolic garbo (earthen lamp/pot) - life’s energy at the center.

ShaktiAmbe MaaVijayadashamiGarbo (lamp)

Why we do Garba

Circles symbolize the cosmos - no beginning or end. Dancing around the lamp honors the Goddess at the center of life. The claps mark rhythm and community; the circle makes everyone a participant. It’s devotion (bhakti), cardio, and community - all in one.

The Nine Days of Navratri

Forms of the Goddess often honored in Gujarat (colors vary by tradition/region).

Day 1: Shailaputri

Daughter of the mountains; purity and purpose. Color: Yellow.

Day 2: Brahmacharini

Tapas (penance), devotion. Color: Green.

Day 3: Chandraghanta

Courage, grace in adversity. Color: Gray.

Day 4: Kushmanda

Cosmic creative energy. Color: Orange.

Day 5: Skandamata

Motherly compassion. Color: White.

Day 6: Katyayani

Strength and resolve. Color: Red.

Day 7: Kalaratri

Fierce protector; destroys fear. Color: Royal Blue.

Day 8: Mahagauri

Serenity and purity. Color: Pink.

Day 9: Siddhidatri

Blessings and fulfillment. Color: Purple.

Garba 101

Dance with rhythm, respect, and joy.

Steps

Start with be-taali (two-clap), try dodhiyu (spin-and-clap), move to tran-taali.

  • ✓ Join from the outer edge; match the tempo.
  • ✓ Gentle taps in dandiya; keep spacing.
  • ✓ Hydrate, light fabrics, comfy shoes.
